What does each part of the chart mean?

Each cell in the 3 × 3 grid is a palace (宮) tied to a compass direction. South sits at the top by tradition. The numbers in the corners are the Luo Shu (洛書) magic-square numbers for each palace. Within every cell, the chart stacks five layers of meaning — read top to bottom: Spirit (八神) → Star (九星) → Door (八門) → Heaven Plate stem (天) → Earth Plate stem (地). The Spirit and Star say what kind of energy is in that direction; the Door says what action that direction supports or blocks; the two stems say what raw material the direction is made of.

Why some palaces have a bold outline

Two of the nine palaces carry special status in every chart and are marked with bold inset borders on the grid. Read the chart at a glance by starting with the bold-outlined palace — whatever spirit + door + stems landed there is the moment's primary signal. The other 8 palaces describe the surrounding terrain.

值符 palace — outer ring (focal point)
The palace where the 值符 (Director / Zhi Fu) star sits. This is the moment's energetic focal point — the strongest auspicious anchor in the chart, where the moment's primary energy is concentrated.
值使 palace — inner ring (active expression)
The palace where the 值使 (Zhi Shi) door sits. This is where action expresses most strongly in this moment — the active door driving how the moment unfolds.
Both in the same palace — double ring
Common in many charts. That cell gets a doubly-bold outline (outer ring for 值符, inner ring for 值使). Treat it as the chart's primary signal — the focal point and the active expression collapsing onto one direction.

What time-basis is this chart? (時家 / hour-based)

This is an hour-based QiMen Dunjia cast — known as 時家奇門 (the most common form in modern practice). The chart is anchored to the hour pillar (時柱), which means it changes every two-hour shichen (時辰). Two casts on the same day at different hours will give different Heaven Plate stems, doors, stars, and spirits — even though the Ju (局) and Earth Plate stay the same within a yuan of a solar term.

Consulting multiple times a day: just recast at each question. Because the chart rotates per shichen, a 9 AM cast and a 3 PM cast on the same day are structurally different — the same way 時家 practitioners read them. There is also a separate day-based tradition (日家奇門) that produces one chart per day for a "shape of today" reading; that's not what this oracle currently casts. If you want day-based readings, that would be a separate engine — flag it and we can build it as a toggle.

Header line (above the grid)

節氣 / 三元
Solar term and yuan (上 / 中 / 下). Together they pick the Ju — every moment within a yuan of a solar term shares the same chart base.
局 (e.g. 陽遁4局)
The chart number 1–9 plus 陽 (yang) or 陰 (yin) dunjia. Determines the Earth Plate layout and the direction of flying.
四柱
Year / month / day / hour pillars in stem-branch form. The hour pillar (時柱) is the most relevant for QMDJ — it drives the Heaven Plate rotation.
值符 / 值使
The "Director" star and door for this moment, with the palace each occupies. The 值符 palace concentrates the moment's energy; the 值使 palace is where action expresses most strongly.
飛步
Number of palaces the Heaven Plate has rotated relative to the Earth Plate. 0 means the chart is "still" — slow change. Larger values indicate more dynamic moments.

It is not authoritative — a practitioner reading would also factor in 五行 generation/control between palaces, 反吟 / 伏吟 patterns, and the nature of the specific question. Notable patterns to look for

  • 三奇 + 三吉門 in the same palace — a Three Wonder (乙/丙/丁) sitting with an auspicious door (開/休/生). Strong window for outward action in that direction.
  • 庚 (Geng) collisions — wherever 庚 lands, treat with caution. 庚 marks blockage and conflict; pairing it with the day stem or 值符 amplifies the warning.
  • Death door (死門) on the day stem — the direction associated with the day stem carrying 死門 is where loss tends to surface; read carefully.
  • 值符 palace alignment — note which palace the 值符 sits in. That direction concentrates the moment's primary energy; pair it with the door in that palace to judge what action that energy supports.
